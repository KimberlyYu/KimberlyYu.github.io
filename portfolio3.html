Arcadia High Mobile App

After almost a year of work, the AHS Mobile App was successfully launched on August 13, 2020. The newly redesigned app provides Arcadia students, parents, and staff access to important school information and resources. Currently, we have 775 daily users and put out 30 articles every week combined with each article's own push notifications. The front-end of the iOS app was built using Swift while the Android app was made with Java. We implemented a personal barcode, unique to each student, expediting all teachers' daily routines of signing students in and out of class. There's also an added benefit of maintaining a smaller carbon footprint by eliminating the use of plastic for ID cards.

Firebase Realtime Database CRUD Web Application

In order to input articles, remove articles, and send notifications to the user's phone through the app, we needed a website that could create, read, update, and delete articles for our news app. Thus, my twin sister and I created the AHS Content Editing Website using HTML, CSS, Javascript, and JQuery. We used Firebase's authentication, hosting, and notification services to sign users in and out, deploy and host our website, and send push notifications to the many people in Arcadia who use the app.

UCSC COSMOS - Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing Cluster

Under professors Narges Norouzi and Snigdha Chaturvedi, I worked with 3 other members to predict the prices of homes with 28 different price-relative features in Ames, Iowa using Multivariate Linear Regression. Gradient Descent was used to get closer to the local minimum. Had an accuracy of 81% after 250 epochs.
